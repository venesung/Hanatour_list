Personal Information PRocessing Policy

Hanatour Service Inc. (hereinafter referred as the 'Company') makes the best endeavors to protect the personal information of a client and guarantee the rights and interests of an information owner. The Company establishes the 'Personal Information Processing Policy' and complies with it in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Act on the Protection of Personal Information' and the standards of the 'Promotion of Information and Communications Network Utilization and Information Protection Act'. It is informed as follows as to what kind of purpose and procedure the personal information of a client is collected and used and what kind of measures are taken in order to protect the personal information. In the event of revising this Personal Information Processing Policy, the Company will provide advance notice through the Company's website.

1. Purpose for the Collection and Use of Personal Information

(1) The Company collects the minimum essential information from clients for the purpose of conducting the processing of business, such as the reservation of travel packages and offers of travel-related services, and shall not use such collected personal information for any purpose other than any applicable purpose without the prior consent of a client.
(2) In the case of revising any collection items, the purpose of use and the usage of personal information, the Company shall provide advance notice and try to obtain consent from the client.
(3) The Company may use any collected personal information for the following purposes:

A. Fulfillment of contracts regarding the offer of service and payment of fares according to the offer of service

Reservation of travel packages, applications for travel insurance, reservation of airline tickets/hotels, and confirmation of immigration information, confirmation and consultation of reservation details, provision of content, special treatment of membership in cases of using travel services, accumulation/inquiry/use of mileage and guidance thereof, issuance/purchase/fare settlement of membership cards, delivery of commodities or invoice forwarding, subscriber accreditation and banking services, etc.

2. Items and Methods of Personal Information Collection

(1) The Company has collected the personal information of a client, to the extent of the minimum information required for the use of services by the client, in accordance with due process and legal standards. The collection and use of the personal information are restricted under the provisions of the Information & Communications Network Act and the Act on the Protection of Personal Information.
(2) The Company shall not collect any sensitive personal information items (race, religion, ideology, political orientation, physical condition, sexual life information, etc.) which may relate to any potential infringement of a client's human rights.

6. Matters Concerning the Automatic Collection Apparatus of Personal Information

The Company may use any 'cookie' to provide the personalized service of a user. The 'cookie' refers to a small quantity of information that a website sends to the computer browser (Netscape, Explorer, etc.) of a user. When a user logs onto a website, a server reads the contents of the cookie, contained in the browser of the client, and finds any additional information from the computer of the client itself to provide such additional information. This cookie information may be used to provide useful and more convenient customized service to a user.
The cookie is used for any of the following purposes:

(1) If a member is registered, a cookie is set up in the case of the first log-in and is deleted in the case of a log-out. A cookie relating to the log-in of a member is used for the purpose of checking whether the current time is in a logged-in status.
(2) A cookie is used in the set-up of a user environment. After the search service environment values as selected directly by a user are stored, such information is used for the purpose of setting the environments necessary for the user.
(3) Any information regarding a user's usage behavior, such as the difference of access frequency or the degree of repeating usages between members and non-members, is determined so as to use it for any target marketing purposes.
(4) A cookie is used for the purpose of checking the participation frequency of a client in various promotions, such as any events held by Hanatour Service Inc.
(5) A user can receive all cookies by adjusting the option of its web browser or have a notice sent when a cookie is installed, or reject all cookies. The methods are as follows:

A. When using Internet Explorer
 - Click [Internet Option] in [Tools] menu.
 - Click the [Personal Information] tab.
 - Adjust the setting-up level of personal information by a user.

B. When using Netscape
 - Click the [Environment Setting-up in [Edit] menu.
 - Click [Cookie] from the [Personal Information Security] category.
 - Select the desired cookie set-up.

7. Rights and Obligations of Personal Information Owners

A personal information owner can exercise his/her rights relating to the protection of their personal information, as provided for in any of the following sub-paragraphs, to the Company at any time:
1) Request for the perusal of personal information (perusal of the source, processing purpose, and usage details of personal information as collected from an information owner and any person other than the information owner)
2) Request for a correction in the case of any errors, etc.
3) Request for the deletion of personal information
4) Request for the suspension of processing

The exercise of rights relating to the above items may be made to the Company in writing, by telephone, e-mail, or FAX, etc. and the Company will take immediate action.
An information owner has an obligation in which the personal information and privacy of both the information owner itself, as handled by the Company, and other persons are not infringed to a level violating relevant laws and regulations, such as the Act on the Protection of Personal Information.

In the case of children under 14 years of age, he/she may make a contract to use travel packages/services through his/her legal representative (parent, legal representative, or a person with parental rights) and his/her minimum personal information required for the offer of applicable services shall be collected. The legal representative of children under 14 years of age can request to peruse and correct the personal information of their children or withdraw any consent to collect and provide and consign the personal information. The personal information of children under 14 years of age shall not be provided to or shared with any third party without the approval of the legal representative.

11. Measures to Secure Technical/Managerial Safety Regarding Personal Information Protection

(1). Technical Measures
- The personal information of a client is protected with a password. Any important data is protected through a separate security function by encrypting any files and transmission data or using a file-locking function.
- The Company has taken measures for the prevention of any damage, as caused by any computer virus, using a vaccine program. The vaccine program is updated periodically and, in the case of any sudden appearance of a virus, a new vaccine program is provided immediately after its provision, so as to prevent the infringement of personal information.
- The Company has adopted a security system (SSL or SET) to transmit safely any personal information on a network by using an encryption algorithm.
- The Company makes its best efforts in terms of security by using a firewall and a vulnerability analysis system in each server in order to provide against any outside intrusion.

(2). Managerial Measures
The Company limits its right to access the personal information of a client to the minimum number of people. Such persons fall under any of the following:
- A person carrying out any business relating to the reservation/payment of travel packages and service fulfillment contracts
- A person conducting any marketing business activities directly to any user
- A person conducting a personal information management business, such as a managing supervisor and a person in charge of personal information management
- Where personal information processing is unavoidable because of other business activities
- Regular in-house training and external consignment training regarding the acquisition of new security technology and the obligations of protecting personal information are conducted among employees handling personal information.
- Any information leak by any person is pre-emptively prevented by securing a security oath from all employees when they join the Company. Internal procedures are prepared for the purpose of auditing the fulfillment matters of the Personal Information Processing Policy and the employee's compliance to the Policy.
- The taking-over responsibilities of a person in charge of personal information are thoroughly conducted in a state in which security is fully maintained. Any liability against a personal information accident in joining and resigning the Company is made clear.
- Personal information and general data are not mixed, but stored after separation through a server.
- A computer room and a data storage room are set up as special protection areas to control their entrance and exit.
- The Company shall not be responsible for any matters resulting from the personal error of a user or the fundamental risks of data transmission of the internet. Members shall be responsible for managing properly their IDs and passwords in order to protect their personal information.
- In the event of any loss, leakage, falsification, and/or damage of personal information due to the error of an internal manager or technical management, the Company shall immediately notify users of such said facts and seek to take proper measures and provide compensation.

12. Personal Information Destruction Management

After the purpose of using any collected personal information is achieved, the Company shall immediately destroy any applicable information in accordance with the usage and retention period. The destruction procedure and method are as follows:

A. Destruction Procedure
Any personal information which the client provides for the registration of membership and the use of travel services shall be stored during a fixed period in accordance with the Company's internal policy and the information protection purposes pursuant to the provisions of other relevant laws and regulations after its purpose of use is achieved and it is thereafter deleted or destroyed.

B. Destruction Method
- Personal information printed on paper: pulverization using a pulverizer
- Personal information stored in an electronic file: personal information is not retained and its records are deleted through a method in which such records cannot be regenerated

C. Destruction Period
- In case of membership registration information: when the membership is withdrawn or expelled
- In case of payment information: when payment is completely made or the prescription period of credit extinction is expired
- In the case of collected personal information for the purpose of conducting surveys and events: when such surveys and events are terminated

Provided, however, that, even though the collection or provision purpose of personal information is achieved, in the case of any necessity to keep personal information under the provisions of laws and regulations such as the Commercial Law, the personal information of a member may be retained.
